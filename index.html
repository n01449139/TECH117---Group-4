Introduction

The project aims to build a simple electronic piano using two Arduino Uno boards. Our project uses multiple push buttons as piano keys, a and connects them to two passive buzzers. When a key is pressed, it shall play a single piano note, and corresponding LEDs will provide visual feedback to the buzzer audio that will be triggered by the buttons.

Success Criteria

  • The circuit works reliably, and every button plays the correct note.
  • The sound from both buzzers is clear, and there is minimal latency in the playback.
  • We are able to form melodies by pressing the keys in sequence.

Limitations and Risks

  • Time: We have about three weeks as of writing this to finalize our project.
  • Money: As we are using two arduino units, we will have to be conservative with how much we spend on the other parts of our project.
  • Parts: The buzzer we are using may give us some issues when trying to output the specific frequencies we desire, and the sound may have some latency
  • Alternative Plans: If we have trouble integrating the two arduino units, we will have to condense our prokect to one unit, limiting the range of notes we will have. Alternatively, if the two buzzers do not work well together, we can switch to using only one speaker.

Environmental Impact Assessment

  • Materials: The Arduino Uno is RoHS and WEEE compliant, limiting hazardous substances.
  • Energy Efficiency: The circuit draws minimal power (~50 mA at 5V), ideal for USB or solar power.
  • Reusability: All components are modular and reusable, reducing electronic waste.
  • Packaging & Shipping: Ordering all components from a single supplier helps minimize carbon footprint.
